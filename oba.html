Mission to Obanlado & Orogbo

Obanlado was completely Islamic when we first arrived. Orogbo, the larger village, had a mix of Muslims and idol worshippers. While Obanlado is predominantly Yoruba, Orogbo includes people from Yoruba, Benin Republic, and northern Nigeria, especially during the farming season.

In 2020, we found that several churches had tried and failed to plant a church in Orogbo. They had given up. We made multiple outreach attempts in 2020, 2021, and 2022 with no lasting result. But in 2023, during our student mission training, God directed us to nearby Obanlado—and the outcome was different.

Breakthrough in Obanlado

Despite few conversions during the student-led outreach, we knew a church plant was needed. The village chief (Baale) supported us—much to the dismay of the Imam, who continues to resist our work.

By God’s grace, the church in Obanlado is growing. Some people from Orogbo are also now joining us. Their response when we visit is heartfelt and persistent:

“Please, we were the first. You people should not abandon us.”

“Please don’t build your church in Obanlado.”

“We will give you land here.”

Yet our plans to purchase two plots in Obanlado for a church building are ongoing. We continue to remind them: the church is not just a building—it’s the people of God in community.

Our Focus: Evangelism & Discipleship

Whether in Obanlado, Orogbo, Olugbo, or surrounding settlements, all believers who attend the SOL-planted church are one in Christ. Our preferred approach is one-on-one evangelism and house-to-house discipleship, bringing the gospel directly to the people where they live.
