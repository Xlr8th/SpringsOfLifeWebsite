Owudekudu Mission Field

A story of total obedience, clarity, and belief in God’s transformative power.

Support the Mission

Breaking Ancient Strongholds

Owudekudu was once a stronghold of idolatry, ancestral worship, and witchcraft. When an Evangelist first brought our lead missioner, Henry Imogbore Oise to the village, he was surprised that a community so close to Abeokuta had no church. We soon found out why.

During our first outreach, village elders asked us to suspend the program for a politician from Abuja to perform his annual sacrifice. The locals even bragged about their “church-resistant” heritage—citing stories of church planters who died, a child who passed away, and even a pastor’s wife who went blind. But we were not deterred.

In the first three months, we had just one church member. Three more months passed and another joined. But by year’s end, God had drawn many into the fold.

Vision & Calling

In December 2009, SOL entered Owudekudu alongside an evangelist. After three days, the evangelist walked away — leaving behind new converts and no church.

SOL stepped in with faith and obedience. In February 2010, the Lord gave Henry Oise a vision saying, “…this is little London. I will bring great transformation to this place.” And God has been faithful to that word.

Transformation Through Obedience

From a dense forest with few homes, Owudekudu is now a growing town with new structures, incoming settlers, and increasing government interest — all signs of God’s promise being fulfilled.

Through SOL's commitment to discipleship, idol worship and witchcraft have lost their hold. The community is being spiritually and socially transformed.

New Life Academy

Launched in April 2013, New Life Academy is a tuition-free school in Owudekudu. With over 153 pupils from nine villages, it has attracted attention from the government.

Many children travel long distances to learn. This school is a testament to what obedience and faith can build.

Vision: Vocational Secondary School

A new step of faith is underway — a vocational secondary school to equip children with practical skills for today’s world.

This school will provide sustainable education and transformation in rural communities.
