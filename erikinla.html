Advancing against all odds—bringing light into a spiritually dark region.

About Eriki-nla

Eriki-nla is a remote and spiritually fortified village with no electricity and barely accessible roads. The people are known for their devotion to Igunnuko (masquerade) worship, serving as dancers to kings, including the Alake of Egbaland.

Though they speak Yoruba, they also have a distinct language—Tapa—used in daily life and for spiritual masquerade rituals. The village remains socially closed, forbidding sexual restraint and only marrying within their community.

Evangelism Effort

Since our outreach began in 2019, we've experienced steady resistance. However, by God's grace, our persistence has yielded fruit. Through open-air meetings, power encounters, and patient follow-up, lives are being transformed.

We follow up with converts through personal home visits until they are confident enough to identify publicly with the faith community. Weekly Bible studies are now held in Eriki-nla, Akeju, and Elepo.

Progress & Hallelujah Land

After years of opposition, a breakthrough came when a prominent masquerade leader was converted. We were able to purchase three plots of land, followed by three more. This area is now called Hallelujah Land.

We worship openly there, and plans are underway to erect a church building and a health center that will serve the surrounding communities.

A Growing Community

Our church now welcomes members not just from Eriki-nla, but also from neighboring villages like Awonke, Elepo, Boto, and Akeju. What began as a mission field is becoming a mission hub.
